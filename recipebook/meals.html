-Butter Chicken-

A rich and creamy Indian dish featuring tender chicken pieces in a tomato-based sauce, often flavored with aromatic spices and finished with butter and cream.

-Ingredients-

For the Marinade

  • 500 grams boneless chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • salt to taste

For the Gravy

  • 2 tablespoons butter and 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts (soaked in warm water)

-Process-

STEP 1 : Marinating the Chicken

In a bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon
juice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well, ensuring each piece is coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

STEP 2 : Cooking the Chicken

Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Add a little oil to prevent sticking. Place the marinated chicken pieces on the pan and cook until they are browned and cooked through, about 8-10 minutes. Set aside.

STEP 3 : Preparing the Butter Chicken Sauce

In a blender, combine the soaked cashew nuts and water. Blend until you have a smooth cashew paste. Set aside. In a large pan or skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent and golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add the red chili powder, garam masala, ground coriander, and ground cumin. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously. Pour in the tomato puree and simmer for about 5 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the sauce. Add the cashew paste and heavy cream. Stir well to combine. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little water to achieve the desired consistency. Add the cooked chicken pieces to the sauce and simmer for another 10 minutes. Make sure the chicken is heated through and absorbs the flavors of the sauce. Season with salt to taste.Garnish the butter chicken with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, rice, or your choice of bread.

Enjoy your homemade butter chicken! Adjust the spice level and creaminess to your liking, and feel free to add more butter if desired.

-Mutton Biryani-

A popular and flavorful Indian dish made with chicken, rice, and a blend of aromatic spices.

-Ingredients-

For the Marinade

  • 500 grams boneless chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • salt to taste

For the rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4-5 cups water for boiling rice
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani Gravy

  • 2 large onions,sliced and 2 tomatoes
  • 2 green chilies and 1/4 cup fresh coriander
    leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves,and 2 tablespoons
    cooking oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds,fennel seeds,
    coriander powder,red chilli powder,turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1 tablespoon of butter

-Process-

STEP 1 : Marinating the Chicken

Combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala,salt and coat the chicken evenly. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.

STEP 2 : Preparing Rice

In a large pot, bring 4-5 cups of water to a boil. Add the washed rice to the boiling water along with green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt. Cook the rice until it's about 70-80% cooked. Drain the rice and set it aside.

STEP 3 :Biryani Gravy

In a blender, combine the soaked cashew nuts and water. Blend until you have a smooth cashew paste. Set aside. Heat oil and ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or a biryani pot.Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Let them splutter. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil begins to separate. Add coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, biryani masala powder, and salt. Stir well and cook for 2-3 minutes.Add marinated chicken pieces and cook. Add fresh coriander and mint leaves.Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Drizzle a little ghee over the rice.

STEP 4 :Dum Cooking(Slow Cooking)

In a blender, combine the soaked cashew nuts and water. Blend until you have a smooth cashew paste. Set aside. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with a dough made of flour and water. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked and the rice is tender. Remove from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the chicken biryani with a fork, mixing the rice and chicken together.

Serve your delicious homemade chicken biryani hot with raita or a side salad. Enjoy your meal!.

-Shahi Egg Curry-

A rich and creamy Indian dish that combines boiled eggs with a flavorful tomato-based sauce.

-Ingredients for the Egg Curry-

  • 6 Eggs, 2 large onions, finely chopped and 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream and 1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste and 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your preference) and 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds and and 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon powder and 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg powder
  • salt to taste and fresh coirander for garnish

-Process-

STEP 1 : Preparing Shahi Curry Sauce

In a blender, grind the soaked cashew nuts into a smooth paste with a little water.Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and allow them to splutter. Add chopped onions and saute until they become translucent and golden brown.Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears. Stir in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, cardamom powder, cinnamon powder, nutmeg powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add the cashew nut paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.Reduce the heat to low and whisk in the yogurt and fresh cream. Cook for a few more minutes until the sauce thickens and is well combined. Add some water to achieve the desired consistency of the sauce. Adjust the salt and spices according to your taste.

STEP 2 : Cooking Eggs in the Shahi Curry Sauce

Crack the eggs directly into the simmering Shahi curry sauce, making sure to space them out evenly. Cover the pan and let the eggs cook in the sauce on low heat for about 8-10 minutes or until the egg whites are set, and the yolks are still slightly runny (you can cook them longer if you prefer hard yolks). Sprinkle salt and pepper over the eggs as they cook.

STEP 3 : Garnish and Serve

Garnish your Shahi Egg Curry with fresh coriander leaves. Serve the curry hot with naan, rice, or roti.

Enjoy your Shahi Egg Curry with eggs cooked directly in the creamy gravy!.

-Thandoori Chicken-

A popular Indian dish known for its smoky and flavorful taste.

-Ingredients-

For the Marinade

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or 8 bone-in
    chicken pieces.
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice and 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika

  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red
    chili powder
  • Salt and Freshly ground black pepper to taste

    • For the Garnish

  • Lemon Wedges
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Onion rings

-Process-

STEP 1 : Prepare the Chicken

If using boneless chicken breasts, cut them into 1-inch cubes or strips. If using bone-in chicken, make shallow cuts on the pieces to help the marinade penetrate.

STEP 2 : Marinate the Chicken

Combine all the ingredients for the marinade and mix thoroughly until it's smooth and well combined.Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and coat them well. Make sure the chicken is well covered with the marinade. You can use your hands for this, but make sure they are clean. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight for the best flavor.

STEP 3 : Preheat Your Grill or Oven

If using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat. If using an oven, preheat it to the highest temperature setting (usually around 450¬∞F or 230¬∞C) and set it to the broil or grill function.

STEP 4 : Cook the Chicken

Garnish your Shahi Egg Curry with fresh coriander leaves. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers (if using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes beforehand to prevent burning). Place the skewers on the grill or on a baking tray if using the oven. Grill or broil the chicken for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until it's cooked through and has a nice charred appearance. The exact cooking time may vary depending on your grill or oven, so keep an eye on it.Serve hot with naan bread, rice, or your favorite Indian side dishes like raita and mint chutney.

Enjoy your homemade hot and spicy tandoori chicken!

-Naan-

A soft and fluffy Indian flatbread that's traditionally cooked in a tandoor (clay oven), or on the stovetop.

-Ingredients for Naan-

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour.
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder and baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt.
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt and 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons melted ghee or butter
  • 3/4 cup warm milk (approximately)
  • Additional melted ghee or butter for brushing (optional)
  • Chopped fresh cilantro or garlic (optional, for garnish)

-Process-

STEP 1 : Making the Dough

In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.In another bowl, mix the yogurt, sugar, and melted ghee or butter.Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and start mixing. Slowly add warm milk, and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough. The exact amount can vary based on factors like humidity and the type of flour you use. Once the dough is formed, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest in a warm place for about 2-3 hours.

STEP 2 : Pre-heating the oven

After the resting period, the dough should have doubled in size. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature setting (usually around 500¬∞F or 260¬∞C) and place a pizza stone or a heavy baking sheet in the oven while it's preheating.

STEP 3 : Shaping and baking Dough

Divide the dough into equal-sized portions and roll them into balls. Take one ball of dough and flatten it with your hands or a rolling pin into an oval or round shape, about 1/4 inch thick. You can use some dry flour to prevent sticking. If you want, you can brush the top of the naan with melted ghee or butter and sprinkle chopped cilantro or garlic for added flavor.Carefully transfer dough to hot pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven. Bake for about 2-3 minutes until the naan gets golden brown spots on top.

Enjoy your homemade naan! It's a wonderful accompaniment to curries, stews, or as a snack with chutney or yogurt.

-Dal Tadka-

A flavorful Indian lentil dish that's commonly served as a side dish with rice or Indian bread.

-Ingredients-

For the Tadka

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seed , 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds,
    1/2 teaspoon asafoetida
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic and 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder and 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish and Lemon wedges (optional)

For the Dal

  • 1cup of yellow or red lentils.
  • 4 cup of water.
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt to taste

-Process-

STEP 1 : Praparing Dal

Rinse the lentils thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. In a large saucepan or pressure cooker, add the rinsed lentils, 4 cups of water, turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook for about 3-4 whistles until the lentils are soft and mushy. If using a regular saucepan, cover and simmer until the lentils are tender (approximately 20-25 minutes), adding more water if needed. Once the lentils are cooked, use a whisk or a ladle to mash them until smooth. Add more water if you prefer a thinner consistency. Keep the dal aside.

STEP 2 : Making tadka

In a separate pan, heat ghee or vegetable oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter. Add asafoetida (hing) and saut√© for a few seconds. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent. Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate. Add the red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for a couple of minutes, stirring constantly. Pour this tempering mixture (tadka) into the cooked dal and mix well. Let it simmer for a few more minutes to blend the flavors. Add salt to taste and adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice if desired.

Your dal curry is ready to be served! Serve it hot with steamed rice or Indian bread. A comforting and nutritious dish that's loved by many.

-Channa Masala-

A popular North Indian dish made with chickpeas cooked in a flavorful tomato-based gravy.

-Ingredients-

For the masala

  • 2 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seed , 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds,
    1/2 teaspoon asafoetida
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped.
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic and 1-inch piece of ginger, minced.
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice preference).
  • 1 large tomato, finely chopped.
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste).
  • 1 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes or 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder and 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish and Lemon wedges (optional)

For the spice blend

  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds and 1 teaspoon cumin seeds.
  • 2-3 whole dried red chilies.

-Process-

STEP 1 : Making the Spice blend

Heat a dry skillet or pan over medium-low heat. Add the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and dried red chilies for the spice blend. Roast them for 2-3 minutes until they become aromatic. Be careful not to burn them. Remove the roasted spices from the heat and allow them to cool. Then, grind them into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set the spice blend aside.

STEP 2 : Praparing the gravy

In a large skillet or pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and bay leaf. Saut√© for a minute until the cumin seeds start to splutter. Add the finely chopped onions and saut√© until they become soft and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to

separate. Add the roasted spice blend (coriander-cumin-red chili powder), coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly, until the spices are well incorporated. Add the cooked chickpeas and mix well, coating them with the spice mixture. Pour in about 1 to 1.5 cups of water (adjust to your desired consistency). Bring it to a simmer, cover, and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the gravy to thicken. Add garam masala and dried mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice. Adjust the salt and spice level according to your taste. Simmer for a few more minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve your Chana Masala hot with rice, naan, or roti.

Enjoy your homemade Chana Masala! It's a delightful and hearty vegetarian dish that's loved by many.
